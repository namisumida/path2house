COLLEGE

Overall, half of all House members attended a public university/college for an undergraduate degree, while a third attended a private college and about one-in-ten (11%) attended an elite school. And while a vast majority received a Bachelor's degree, a small portion (6%) have not.

Some party differences emerge when it comes to undergraduate school type. Democratic reps are more likely to have attended private and elite schools (by 11 and 9 percentage-points, respectively) and less likely to have attended public schools (by 17 points).

GRADUATE SCHOOL

As expected, a majority of all members (69%) have continued on to higher education. Roughly a third have obtained a law degree (37%) or a master's (34%), while very few have received a medical degree (5%) or a PhD (4%).

Democratic reps are more likely to have gone to law school (by 12 points) and obtained a masters (by 10 points), but Republicans are more likely to have gone to medical school (by 6 points).

Interestingly, some of the few without Bachelor's degrees are with higher-education degrees. Two have a law degree and another a medical degree.
